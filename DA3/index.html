  • High Concept: A one-sentence summary of your game.
    Celebrity cartoonist John Carbuckle just wants to do his laundry and draw his cartoons, but his cats won't let him. Try to do your laundry, draw your cartoons, and keep your curtains closed while your cats try to stop you.

    I want players to feel like they are John, and they just want to do their laundry and work on their cartoons in private

You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
    Move with arrow keys, space bar to close curtains, interact with washer/dryer, and draw cartoons at the desk.
    If the curtains are open, your privacy goes down. If they are closed, your privacy goes up. If you run out of privacy, game over.
    You want to finish your laundry. The laundry can only be completed if the washer/dryer is actually running. When the washer is done, move the clothes to the dryer.
    You also want to work on drawing your cartoons. Drawing cartoons increases your score.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    If the player is low on privacy, they may wish to stay near the window until their privacy recovers.
    If the curtains are closed and the laundry is running, the player may wish to stay at the desk and work on cartoons to increase their score.
